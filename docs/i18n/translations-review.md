# DoubleDone — translation review (Italian · French · Spanish)

**For the native-speaker review.** These are *draft* translations of DoubleDone's first set of strings, ready
for a vibe-check. You do not need to know anything technical to review them. Read the Italian / French / Spanish
next to the English, and where something sounds **stiff, cold, too formal, or just not how a person would say
it**, mark it or suggest a better line. Your ear is the final word here.

## What DoubleDone sounds like

It is a calm, gentle to-do app for people who get overwhelmed (ADHD, autism, OCD). The voice is **warm,
reassuring, plain, and decisive** — never clinical, never pushy, never cute or jokey. It talks to you like a kind
friend, so every language here uses the **informal "you"** (*tu* / *tu* / *tú*), never the formal register.

A few feature names are **idioms** ("Lighten today", "Break it down", "Make it tiny", "Chart a course"). These
are **transcreated**, not translated literally — we look for the phrase a real app in that language would use for
the same calm idea. Those are the ones most worth your eye. The rationale for each is at the bottom.

## Why the idioms read the way they do (the transcreation notes)

- **Lighten today** → *Alleggerisci oggi* / *Allège ta journée* / *Aligera el día.* All three mean "lighten the
  load," lifting weight off the day, which is exactly what the feature does (it moves a few tasks to later).
  Literal "brighten/clarify" (*illuminare*, *éclaircir*) would be wrong.
- **Break it down** → *Spezzala in passi* / *Décompose-la* / *Divídela en pasos.* "Split it into steps." The very
  literal "break" (*rompi*, *rompe*) sounds violent for a task; these are the calm verbs a real app uses.
- **Make it tiny** → *Rendila minuscola* / *Juste un petit bout* / *Hazla mínima.* The feature makes a dreaded
  task into a two-minute starter. The French leans into the reassurance ("just a small piece") rather than a
  literal "make it tiny," which sounds odd in French.
- **Chart a course** → *Traccia la rotta* / *Trace ta route* / *Traza un plan.* Italian keeps the calm nautical
  image; French uses "plot your path forward"; Spanish goes to "draw up a plan" because the nautical version
  (*traza un rumbo*) reads too dramatic. Open to your preference here.
- **"that's our job"** → *a quello pensiamo noi* / *c'est notre boulot* / *de eso nos encargamos nosotros.* Kept
  warm and colloquial, not the stiff "it is our work."

## How to give feedback

Edit the Italian / French / Spanish cells directly, or jot a note beside any line. Anything that makes you wince,
flag it. Once you're happy, these become the real in-app strings, and we add the in-app language switch so you
can see them live and we widen the set screen by screen.
